### A bit about how I think
I care more about whether something actually helps a person than whether it's technically impressive. The projects below span legal tech, accessibility, and agriculture because that's genuinely what I find interesting — not a strategy. I also try to bring people along with me: I founded Caldwell Robotics to give other students a place to build, and I'd rather mentor someone through a hard bug than just hand them the fix.

When I'm not coding, I'm probably at a hackathon, in the Makerspace, or arguing that the best engineering teams are the ones that trust each other, not just the ones that ship fastest.

### What I'm working on
**GeniusLaw** — I lead a small team building the CRM and AI tools that 500+ attorneys rely on day to day, including a system that turns weeks of drafting into days. **Dristi** — an AI wearable that helps visually impaired users navigate the world, built with a team at Google x NYU. Outside of work, I build precision-agriculture drones and tools, and run Caldwell Robotics, a 70+ member student org I started from scratch.

3x hackathon winner (HackNYU, CalHacks x Anthropic x Amazon, HackNJIT, CougarHacks) and a HackMIT finalist — but the part I'm proudest of is usually who I built it with.
